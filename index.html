Douglas Bader and World War II

How he was made who he is

He was made who he was by his uncle. His uncle was adjutant to the RAF at Cranwell. At the age of eleven, Bader decided to join the RAF.

His role in WWII

Douglas Bader was ace Wing Commander in the Battle of Britain. Douglas was involved iwth a mid-air collision with a German fighter over northern France, parachuting from his destroyed aircraft, getting two artificial legs.

His success during the war

After the second World War had started, Douglas had scored his first victories over Dunkirk during the Battle of France in 1940.
